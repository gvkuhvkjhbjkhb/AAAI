*Deadlock: 1-shot NE is symmetric (D,D), but in the repeated game (horizon=5), DP-Gating's non-intervention allows agents to condition play on history, sustaining the Pareto-superior (C,C) outcome that gated arbitration destroys by forcing alignment.

**Key insight: The Nash equilibrium structure PREDICTS DP-Gating effectiveness with 100% accuracy across all 5 games.** DP-Gating works NOT by "preserving diversity" but by NOT DESTROYING the equilibrium structure required by anti-coordination games. This is a structural claim grounded in game theory.

## 8. Final Verdict

### What the data supports:
1. **Conditional non-intervention robustly improves anti-coordination games** (+0.20~+0.25, p<0.04, 87-100% seed wins across 3 games)
2. **The method is never significantly harmful** (StagHunt neutral p=1.0, BoS n.s. p=0.13, CI crosses zero)
3. **The method is threshold-insensitive** (Chicken range=0.036 across 0.4-0.8)
4. **Nash equilibrium structure predicts effectiveness** (100% match across 5 games)
5. **The mechanism is about intervention timing, not diversity preservation**

### What the data does NOT support:
1. ❌ "Diversity-preserving" — diversity is ~0 for both methods
2. ❌ "Pareto dominance" — no diversity gain means no Pareto frontier
3. ❌ "Conflict rate as game-structure detector" — all games ~0.50

### Recommended paper framing:
**"Less Is More: Selective Non-Intervention Outperforms Forced Alignment in Anti-Coordination LLM Games"**

### AAAI probability: 35-40%
